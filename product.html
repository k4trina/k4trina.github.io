Under the Hood

Every document feeds one connected knowledge graph

A spec section, a trade code, a price — on their own, just facts. Bidblox links them, so when it reads "08 71 00" it already knows the trade, what it cost on similar jobs, and which drawings reference it — enough to catch a mismatched code or a missing scope item instead of transcribing the page.

  • ✓ Trade codes stay consistent across specs, drawings, and bids
  • ✓ Sub bids are leveled against the same scope — gaps and outliers surface on their own
  • ✓ Pricing is benchmarked against what similar projects actually paid
  • ✓ Every node traces back to the document it came from
Why This Matters

We'd rather build understanding than throw answers at you

An estimator won't trust a number they can't trace back to a source — so neither does Bidblox. Every output is built to be checked, not just believed.

1

Reads the source

Reads the real spec pages, drawings, and emails for this project — not a generic template of what a project like this "usually" looks like.

2

Cross-references the graph

Trade codes, leveled bids, and scope all get checked against the graph — so a mismatched code, a scope gap, or a price that's out of line with comparable projects surfaces instead of quietly passing through.

3

Shows its work

Every scope, price, and material links back to the exact spec section or drawing it came from — verify a number in seconds, instead of re-reading the whole document.

4

Flags what it's unsure of

When a document is ambiguous or contradicts itself, Bidblox says so — instead of guessing and presenting a confident-looking answer.
